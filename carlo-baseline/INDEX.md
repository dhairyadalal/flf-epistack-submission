# COVID-19 Origins — Evidence Catalog

> **Authorship.** This whole catalog (this index, the 40 evidence files in `evidence/`, and `CONCLUSION.md`) was researched and written by **Claude**, not by Carlo. It summarizes the public peer-reviewed literature, official reports, and primary documents; it is a reasoned synthesis, not an authoritative scientific verdict.

**How to read this.** Each evidence file states one claim, its key facts, the strongest counterargument, sourced references, and two calibrated scores:
- **claim_confidence (0–1)** — how likely the underlying *factual claim is true*.
- **support_strength (weak/moderate/strong)** — how much the true fact actually *moves the origin question*.

A high-confidence fact can still be weakly probative (e.g. the furin site is real ≈0.97, but near-neutral on origin). The two scores are kept separate on purpose. See `CONCLUSION.md` for how they are weighed.

**Two hypotheses** (stated precisely in `CONCLUSION.md`):
- **Natural zoonotic spillover (H_zoo)** — animal-to-human, most plausibly via the wildlife trade, with the Huanan market as epicenter.
- **Research-related incident (H_lab)** — a natural, passaged, or engineered virus escaped a Wuhan lab (chiefly the WIV). All serious lab variants still require an ancestrally *natural* genome.

**Bottom line (from `CONCLUSION.md`):** ~**72% natural / ~28% lab-related** as a single best estimate, range ~65–80% / 20–35%. Natural origin is better-supported on current *primary* evidence; the lab hypothesis cannot be dismissed and is kept alive mainly by circumstantial/conduct evidence and a split intelligence community. The dominant confound is China's data opacity (symmetric — favors neither side).

## Note on overlapping write-ups

Independent research agents occasionally produced more than one document on the same underlying item. These are **retained, not merged**, because each was sourced independently and the duplicates broaden the citation base. Overlap clusters:

- **(A) Two lineages A/B → multiple spillovers:** `two-zoonotic-introductions-lineages-A-B`, `two-lineages-a-b-multiple-introductions` (and the spatial-clustering angle in `huanan-market-spatial-clustering-two-lineages`).
- **(B) Huanan early-case spatial clustering:** `huanan-market-spatial-clustering-early-cases`, `huanan-market-spatial-clustering-two-lineages`, `absence-of-non-market-early-clustering`.
- **(C) DEFUSE 2018 proposal:** `defuse-grant-proposal-blueprint`, `defuse-2018-furin-cleavage-proposal`, `defuse-furin-preexisting-blueprint`.
- **(D) RaTG13 / Mojiang mine:** `ratg13-mojiang-provenance-secrecy` (lab-leaning framing) and `mojiang-mine-ratg13-provenance` (neutral framing).

In the synthesis these clusters are counted **once each**, not once per document, to avoid double-counting.

*Note: `early-cases-without-market-link` (lab-leaning) and `absence-of-non-market-early-clustering` (natural-leaning) look similar but argue opposite conclusions from the same disputed dataset — they are deliberately kept as a contrasting pair, not a duplicate.*
