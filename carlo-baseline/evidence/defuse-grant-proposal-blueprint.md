# The 2018 DEFUSE proposal proposed inserting furin cleavage sites into novel SARS-like coronaviruses in Wuhan

## Claim
A 2018 grant proposal ("DEFUSE") submitted by EcoHealth Alliance to DARPA, with the Wuhan Institute of Virology (WIV) and UNC's Ralph Baric as collaborators, explicitly proposed assembling chimeric bat SARS-like coronaviruses and inserting "human-specific" proteolytic cleavage sites — including a furin-cleavable site at the S1/S2 spike junction — and testing their growth in human airway cells. DARPA rejected the proposal and there is no documentary evidence the specific experiment was carried out.

## Key facts
- The proposal was real, submitted by EcoHealth Alliance to DARPA in March 2018 under the BAA call "PREEMPT," titled "DEFUSE." Collaborators were EcoHealth (Peter Daszak), WIV (Zhengli Shi), and the Baric lab at UNC Chapel Hill. (The Intercept; U.S. Right to Know)
- The proposal described creating "full-length infectious clones" of bat SARS-related coronaviruses and inserting a "proteolytic cleavage site" able to interact with furin. It stated researchers would "introduce appropriate human-specific cleavage sites and evaluate growth potential in [mammalian cells] and HAE cultures" (human airway epithelial cultures). (The Intercept; U.S. Right to Know, Emily Kopp, Jan 2024)
- The proposed work plan included generating "twenty or more chimeric SARS-related viral spike proteins per year" and "two to five full-length engineered SARS-related viruses," with assembly of synthetic viruses in approximately six genomic segments. (U.S. Right to Know)
- An internal draft comment by Daszak indicated that although the formal proposal assigned spike engineering to Baric in North Carolina, much of the work would in fact be done at WIV — language reportedly arranged to make DARPA "comfortable." (The Intercept)
- DARPA rejected DEFUSE; it was never funded by DARPA. DARPA stated it "has never funded directly, nor indirectly as a subcontractor, any activity or researcher associated with the EcoHealth Alliance or Wuhan Institute of Virology." The rejection cited concerns including gain-of-function risk and handling of pandemic-potential pathogens. (Newsweek, Ed Browne, Sept 2021; DARPA rejection document)
- The documents were obtained via FOIA-adjacent leak and surfaced by the activist group DRASTIC in September 2021; a full draft proposal was later released. (The Intercept; DRASTIC)
- The relevance: SARS-CoV-2 carries a furin cleavage site (PRRA insertion) at the S1/S2 junction — a feature absent from all known close relatives. The proposal describes engineering precisely this class of feature, in the lab nearest the outbreak, ~18 months prior.

## Evidence quality
This is **documentary** evidence: a genuine primary document (the leaked proposal) reported by investigative journalism (The Intercept, U.S. Right to Know) and partially corroborated by DARPA's on-record statements. The existence and contents of the proposal are not seriously disputed — even EcoHealth has acknowledged it. The document is authentic and the quotations are verifiable.

The crucial epistemic gap is the distinction between **a proposal to do X** and **evidence that X was done and produced SARS-CoV-2**. The proposal proves intent and capability circa 2018; it does not prove execution. There is no leaked lab notebook, sequence, or e-mail showing the specific furin-site insertion into a virus resembling SARS-CoV-2 was actually performed. The inference from "they proposed it" to "they did it and it leaked" is circumstantial, not direct.

## Strongest counterargument
Two independent rebuttals weaken the inferential leap:

1. **Not funded, work claimed not done.** DARPA rejected DEFUSE and EcoHealth's Daszak has stated repeatedly that the work was therefore never performed. No documentary evidence shows the furin-insertion experiments were carried out (whether under other funding remains unestablished but unproven).

2. **The actual SARS-CoV-2 furin site does not look engineered the way DEFUSE describes.** Robert Garry (PNAS, 2022, "SARS-CoV-2 furin cleavage site was not engineered") argues the SARS-CoV-2 insertion has signatures inconsistent with deliberate engineering: it is a 12-nucleotide insertion that is *out of frame* relative to a clean codon-optimized design, and the inserted sequence aligns with patterns expected from natural recombination/copy-choice rather than a designer's construct. Virologists (e.g., Vincent Racaniello; Andersen et al.'s "Proximal Origin") note the furin site can arise by natural recombination, as similar insertions exist in other bat coronaviruses. The DEFUSE proposal describes inserting "human-specific" sites but does not specify the exact PRRA/RRAR sequence that SARS-CoV-2 has, so the match is at the level of *concept* (a furin site at S1/S2), not the specific nucleotides. Critics of the engineering hypothesis also note that any ~12-nt stretch is highly likely to contain *some* restriction site, undercutting "engineering fingerprint" arguments.

A fair statement: DEFUSE shows the *idea and the actors* existed; it does not match the *specific molecular details* of SARS-CoV-2 closely enough to function as proof of execution.

## Confidence assessment
- **Claim confidence:** 0.95 — The core factual claim (that DEFUSE existed and proposed inserting human-specific/furin cleavage sites into chimeric bat SARS-like CoVs in collaboration with WIV, and was rejected by DARPA) is very well documented and essentially uncontested. The small residual uncertainty concerns precise wording nuances and whether "human-specific cleavage site" in the proposal is best read as specifically "furin" at S1/S2 in every passage (early drafts are more explicit than the formal version).
- **Support strength:** moderate — For the **lab-leak** hypothesis. It is arguably the strongest single circumstantial item, because it places motive, means, and the distinctive feature in the right place at the right time. But it is downgraded from "strong" because (a) the proposal was rejected and not shown to be executed, (b) the proposed feature is described conceptually rather than matching SARS-CoV-2's exact sequence, and (c) the furin site is now known to be biologically achievable by natural recombination. It raises the prior on lab-leak but does not approach proof.
- **Net:** A calibrated reasoner should treat DEFUSE as genuine, striking, and probative — it materially raises the plausibility of an engineered/lab origin — while recognizing it is a blueprint and not a build record: powerful circumstantial evidence, not a smoking gun.
