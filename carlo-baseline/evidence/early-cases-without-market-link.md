# Some of the earliest documented cases had no link to the Huanan market, and China gave the WHO only case summaries, not raw line-list data

## Claim
A substantial fraction of the earliest documented COVID-19 cases reported no exposure to the Huanan Seafood Market: in Huang et al. (Lancet, Jan 2020) only 27 of the first 41 hospitalized patients (66%) had direct market exposure, and the WHO–China Joint Study (2021) found roughly half of the 174 December-2019 Hubei cases were market-linked (one widely cited extraction is 55 of 168). Separately, a WHO team member (Dominic Dwyer) stated that China provided the WHO mission with case *summaries* rather than the raw "line-list" patient data the team requested.

## Key facts
- **First Lancet-reported case had no market link.** Huang et al. (Lancet, 24 Jan 2020) gave the first patient a symptom-onset date of **1 Dec 2019** and reported **no exposure to the Huanan market** for that individual; the paper also noted no epidemiological link between this first patient and later cases.
- **One-third of the first 41 cases were unlinked.** In the same Huang et al. cohort, **27 of 41 (66%)** had direct Huanan-market exposure — meaning ~34% did not.
- **Roughly half of the WHO's December cases were market-linked.** The WHO–China Joint Study (Mar 2021) identified **174 confirmed cases** in Hubei in December 2019. Dwyer and multiple analyses state that only about half had Huanan exposure; a commonly cited figure from the report's case data is **55 of 168 (~33%)** with a direct market association, with "about half" being the looser characterization given by team members.
- **China withheld raw line-list data.** Dominic Dwyer (Australian infectious-disease physician on the WHO mission) told Reuters/AP/Al Jazeera (Feb 2021) that the team requested **raw patient data ("line listings")** on the 174 cases but received **only a summary**. He said this is standard outbreak-investigation data and that obtaining it mattered precisely *because* only ~half the cases were market-linked.

## Evidence quality
Mixed but mostly solid on the factual core. The case counts come from **peer-reviewed work** (Huang et al., *The Lancet*) and an **official intergovernmental report** (WHO–China Joint Study). The withholding claim is **first-hand testimony from a named WHO team member**, reported by multiple independent outlets (Reuters, AP, Al Jazeera) — credible but single-source and not independently auditable. The precise "55 of 168" figure is a *third-party extraction* of the WHO report's case data rather than a number the report itself foregrounds, so the exact denominator/numerator should be treated as approximate; "about half" is the safest summary.

A key complication: the **onset date of the very first case is itself disputed.** The WHO report listed an 8 Dec 2019 onset for an early case; Worobey (Science, 2021) argued that case (a 41-year-old accountant) actually had COVID onset on **16 Dec**, his Dec-8 complaint being dental, and that the earliest case was instead a **Huanan market vendor** (onset ~11 Dec). A formal correction to that date was issued in Dec 2021. So the "earliest case had no market link" framing depends on which patient is taken as index, and that ordering is contested.

## Strongest counterargument
Worobey et al. (*Science*, 2022, DOI: 10.1126/science.abp8715) argue the market was the genuine epicenter even *after* accounting for ascertainment bias. Their key, somewhat paradoxical finding: early cases with **no direct market link still lived significantly closer to the market** (median ~4.0 km, p<0.001) than the general population — and, strikingly, *closer* than the market-linked cases themselves. They argue this pattern is the opposite of what contact-tracing/ascertainment bias would produce (unlinked cases traced from market clusters would not be expected to cluster *more* tightly around the market). Pekar et al. (*Science*, 2022) add genomic evidence of two early lineages consistent with multiple introductions at the market. On this view, the missing market links and incomplete data reflect imperfect early case-finding, not evidence of an origin elsewhere. Critics of the market hypothesis (e.g., Stoyan & Chiu 2024) dispute the spatial statistics, but the Worobey group has rebutted those critiques (arXiv 2403.05859, 2024).

## Confidence assessment
- **Claim confidence:** 0.9 — The core facts (first Lancet case unlinked, ~34% of first 41 unlinked, ~half of WHO's 174 unlinked, China provided summaries not line lists) are each well-sourced. The deduction is the exact "55 of 168" denominator (a derived figure, not authoritative) and the contested status of *which* case is truly first.
- **Support strength:** weak — for lab-leak. Missing market links and withheld raw data are real and legitimately raise the possibility that the market was an amplifier rather than the source. But they are equally consistent with imperfect early case ascertainment, and the strongest available spatial analysis (Worobey 2022) finds the *unlinked* cases clustered around the market too. The data gap mainly establishes that the timeline is genuinely disputed and incomplete — not that introduction happened elsewhere.
- **Net:** A calibrated reasoner should treat this as solid evidence that the early case data is incomplete and partly withheld, but only a weak update toward lab-leak, because the best reconstruction of the same data still places the geographic center of gravity at the market.
