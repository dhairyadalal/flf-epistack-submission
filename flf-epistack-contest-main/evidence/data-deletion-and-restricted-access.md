# Deleted databases, withdrawn sequences, and restricted WHO access: behavior some read as a cover-up

## Claim
A cluster of data-access events is invoked as evidence of concealment: (1) the Wuhan Institute of Virology's (WIV) main online database of ~22,000 bat/virus sample records and sequences went offline on 12 September 2019, before the recognized outbreak; (2) a set of early Wuhan SARS-CoV-2 deep-sequencing datasets was deleted from the NIH Sequence Read Archive (SRA) at the submitters' request and later recovered by Jesse Bloom; and (3) China declined to provide raw line-list patient data on early cases to the WHO-convened joint mission and restricted aspects of its access. Proponents argue this is the pattern one would expect if there were something to hide.

## Key facts
- **WIV database offline (12 Sep 2019).** The WIV took its principal sample/sequence database offline on 12 September 2019. The figure of ~22,000 entries and the date trace to DRASTIC analysts; the event is documented in European Parliament written question E-000608/2022 (submitted by MEP Joachim Kuhs), which notes the WIV's links to European Virus Archive Global and asks for disclosure. WIV's stated reason (via Shi Zhengli and relayed by Peter Daszak) was that the database faced thousands of hacking attempts and was pulled for security. The timing problem: the takedown predates the recognized outbreak by months, and the "pandemic-era cyberattack" framing offered in some later WIV statements does not fit a September 2019 date (Europarl; Pekingnology; news reporting).
- **Deleted SRA sequences (Bloom 2021).** Jesse Bloom identified that sequencing data from project PRJNA612766 (Wuhan University; nanopore amplicon sequencing of early-epidemic samples) had been deleted from the NIH SRA. He recovered deleted files from Google Cloud and reconstructed partial sequences of 13 early-epidemic viruses, arguing the Huanan market sequences "are not fully representative" of early Wuhan viruses (Bloom 2021, *Molecular Biology and Evolution*). Deletion was made at the submitters' request.
- **Competing accounts of the SRA deletion.** NIH confirmed sequences were withdrawn at the submitting investigator's request; China's National Health Commission later said the journal *Small* had mistakenly removed a data-availability statement, leading authors to delete the SRA data, which were re-uploaded to a Chinese database (NGDC, PRJCA005725) on 8 July 2021. NIH's stated policy posture is that submitters may withdraw data and may resubmit elsewhere.
- **WHO raw-data access.** WHO joint-mission member Dominic Dwyer (and others) said China provided only summaries, not raw "line-list" patient data, on the 174 early December-2019 cases the team requested. This appears in the team's final report and in February 2021 reporting (Al Jazeera/Reuters; CNN). WHO Director-General Tedros later publicly stated the team's data access had been insufficient and called for fuller sharing.
- **GISAID withdrawals (related pattern).** In 2022-2023, Huanan market environmental sequence data uploaded to GISAID were made invisible at the submitter's request after outside researchers began analyzing them (*Science* reporting), an echo of the broader "submitter can un-share" dynamic.

## Evidence quality
Mixed and largely circumstantial. The strongest documented, peer-reviewed piece is Bloom (2021) in *Molecular Biology and Evolution* — the deletion and recovery are real and verifiable. The WHO raw-data dispute is well-attested by named mission participants and appears in the official report. The 12 Sep 2019 database takedown date is real but its provenance (22,000 figure, exact date) rests substantially on DRASTIC analysis and archived snapshots rather than an official audited record; the European Parliament question documents the controversy but does not independently establish motive. Critically, none of these facts is itself evidence about *where* the virus came from — they bear on transparency, not origin.

## Strongest counterargument
Each event has a mundane, non-lab-leak explanation, and the genomic claim has been directly rebutted:
- **The deleted sequences carry little origin signal.** Débarre & Hensel (2025, *Molecular Biology and Evolution*), a critical reexamination, show the recovered sequences were collected on **30 January 2020** — later than hundreds of other sequences — and their mutations are consistent with late-January 2020 diversity. They conclude the data "align with contemporaneous data" and do not support Bloom's inference about an undersampled early progenitor. So even granting the deletion, the recovered content does not point to a lab origin.
- **Withholding is overdetermined by politics, not biology.** Authoritarian opacity, reputational defensiveness, fear of being blamed, and bureaucratic face-saving fully explain database takedowns, withdrawn submissions, and restricted access without any lab-leak premise. The same Chinese reticence applies to data that, when finally analyzed (e.g., the Huanan market environmental samples), has been read by many researchers as *supporting* zoonosis.
- **Submitter-controlled withdrawal is normal database behavior.** SRA and GISAID both let submitters withdraw/un-release data; this routinely happens for resubmission, journal disputes, or QC, and is not unique to COVID.
Concealment lowers confidence in exculpatory Chinese claims, but it is not affirmative evidence for a lab leak.

## Confidence assessment
- **Claim confidence:** 0.9 — The underlying factual events are well-documented and largely uncontested: the 12 Sep 2019 takedown, the SRA deletion and recovery (Bloom), and the WHO raw-data refusal all occurred. The residual uncertainty is in details (exact record count, precise internal motives), not in whether the events happened.
- **Support strength:** weak (for lab-leak) — As behavior-consistent-with-cover-up, it is suggestive but non-dispositive and symmetric: it is equally consistent with a state concealing embarrassing zoonotic-spillover details, regulatory failures, or simply behaving as an opaque autocracy. The one piece with potential genomic content (Bloom) has been substantially neutralized by Débarre & Hensel (2025).
- **Net:** A calibrated reasoner should treat this as real and troubling evidence of obstruction that lowers trust in Chinese exculpatory statements and has genuinely impeded investigation — but should not move it far toward lab-leak, because withholding data is explained at least as well by authoritarian opacity and face-saving as by a specific lab origin.
