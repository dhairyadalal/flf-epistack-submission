# The Mojiang mine, the 2012 miner deaths, and RaTG13's contested provenance

## Claim
RaTG13 — the bat coronavirus ~96.1% identical to SARS-CoV-2 — was sequenced by the Wuhan Institute of Virology (WIV) from a bat-swab sample originally published under the partial-sequence label BtCoV/4991, collected in 2013 from the Tongguan mineshaft in Mojiang, Yunnan, where in 2012 six miners clearing bat guano fell ill with severe pneumonia and three died. WIV did not initially disclose, in its February 2020 *Nature* paper describing RaTG13, that RaTG13 and BtCoV/4991 were the same sample, nor the mine's fatal-illness history; both were disclosed/confirmed only later (Rahalkar & Bahulikar; the November 2020 *Nature* addendum).

## Key facts
- **Genetic distance.** RaTG13 shares **96.1% nucleotide identity** with SARS-CoV-2 across a ~29,800-nt genome — i.e., on the order of ~1,100–1,150 nucleotide differences. Despite being (at publication) the closest known relative, this corresponds to estimated divergence of multiple decades, far too distant to be a direct/proximal ancestor (Zhou et al. 2020; Wikipedia "RaTG13"). It has since been surpassed: **BANAL-52**, found in Laos, is ~96.8% identical.
- **Never isolated.** RaTG13 exists only as a metagenomically reconstructed sequence; **no live RaTG13 virus has ever been isolated** in any laboratory (Wikipedia "RaTG13"; Frutos et al. 2021).
- **The renaming.** The sample was originally Ra4991 / published as **BtCoV/4991** (a short RdRp fragment, 2016), and was renamed **RaTG13** (Ra = *Rhinolophus affinis*, TG = Tongguan, 13 = 2013) when the full genome was reported in 2020. WIV's Feb 2020 paper did not state that the two were the same sample.
- **The mine outbreak.** In April 2012, **six miners** cleaning bat feces in the Tongguan copper mine in Mojiang, Yunnan, developed severe pneumonia; **three died**. Hospitalizations ran from 12 to 137 days (Rahalkar & Bahulikar 2020).
- **The theses.** A 2013 Kunming Medical University master's thesis (Li Xu) concluded the miners' illness was likely caused by a SARS-like coronavirus from horseshoe bats; a later PhD thesis (Canping Huang, Chinese CDC) recorded that serum from the patients was sent to WIV (Rahalkar & Bahulikar 2020; Latham & Wilson 2020).
- **WIV's testing claims.** Per the November 2020 *Nature* addendum, WIV received 13 serum samples from 4 of the patients in 2012; PCR and antibody testing for Ebola, Nipah, and bat SARSr-CoV Rp3 were negative; 2020 ELISA against SARS-CoV-2 nucleocapsid protein was also negative — WIV's stated conclusion: the miners were not infected by SARS-CoV-2 (Zhou et al. 2020 addendum).
- **Disputed cause of death.** Shi Zhengli has variously attributed the deaths to fungal infection (2020 *Scientific American* interview); critics note the original PhD-thesis workup pointed toward a SARS-like CoV. Frutos et al. (2021) argue the clinical picture (hemoptysis, mediastinal lymphadenopathy) is atypical of COVID-19 and more consistent with fungal/hypersensitivity pneumonitis.

## Evidence quality
The core provenance facts are **well-documented and not seriously contested**: the same-sample identity (BtCoV/4991 = RaTG13), the mine location, the 2012 outbreak, and the absence of these details in the original 2020 paper are confirmed by the peer-reviewed Rahalkar & Bahulikar paper (*Frontiers in Public Health*) and by WIV's own peer-reviewed *Nature* addendum. This is primarily **documentary/forensic** evidence about disclosure and labeling, not experimental data. The more speculative layer — that the miners were infected by a SARS-like CoV that was then studied/passaged at WIV (the "Mojiang Miners Passage" hypothesis of Latham & Wilson) — rests on inference from theses and is **not** established; it is a hypothesis, not a finding. Independence is moderate: the disclosure timeline is verifiable from independent parties, but interpretations cluster into advocacy camps.

## Strongest counterargument
The provenance facts do not point to a lab leak, and several cut the other way. (1) **RaTG13 is genetically far too distant** — ~1,100+ nucleotide changes, decades of divergence — to be SARS-CoV-2 or its proximal ancestor, and it has never been isolated as live virus, so there is no demonstrated WIV stock of a SARS-CoV-2-like agent (Frutos et al. 2021; Zhou et al. 2020). (2) **The miners tested negative** for SARS-related CoV in 2012 and for SARS-CoV-2 antibodies in 2020 (per WIV). (3) Frutos et al. (2021), reviewing the clinical records, argue the miners' presentation is **atypical of COVID-19** and better explained by fungal infection or hypersensitivity pneumonitis. On this reading, the renaming/non-disclosure is poor transparency by WIV, not evidence of a concealed leak — sampling at a mine with a prior outbreak is exactly what surveillance virologists would do, and renaming on full-genome publication is ordinary practice.

## Confidence assessment
- **Claim confidence:** 0.92 — The factual claim (same sample, mine provenance, 2012 deaths, non-disclosure in the original paper) is solidly sourced from two peer-reviewed papers including WIV's own addendum. I dock points only because "did not initially disclose" is partly a framing question, and the exact intent behind the renaming is unknowable.
- **Support strength:** weak — For the *origin* question this is weak in either direction. It is a genuine **data-transparency** problem (favoring skepticism of WIV's openness) but provides **no direct evidence of a leak**, and the genetic distance plus negative serology actively undercut the strong lab-leak reading. It neither confirms nor refutes either hypothesis.
- **Net:** A calibrated reasoner should treat this as an established but **low-probative** transparency/credibility issue: real grounds to distrust WIV's initial candor, but not evidence that SARS-CoV-2 came from the Mojiang mine or from WIV — hence neutral.
