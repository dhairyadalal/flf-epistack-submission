# No infected source animal was ever found at the Huanan market or in its supply chain

## Claim
China CDC sampling of 457 animal specimens spanning ~18 species at the Huanan Seafood Market detected SARS-CoV-2 in environmental swabs but in zero animal samples, and subsequent tracing of wildlife farms and supply chains has not identified any infected progenitor or intermediate host animal. Lab-leak proponents cite this persistent absence as a notable evidentiary gap relative to SARS-CoV-1 (civets) and MERS (camels), where infected intermediate hosts were found relatively quickly.

## Key facts
- In the China CDC surveillance study, **923 environmental samples** and **457 animal samples** from **18 species** were collected at and around the Huanan market. SARS-CoV-2 was detected by RT-qPCR in **73 environmental samples** (three live viruses were isolated), but in **none of the animal samples** (Liu et al., *Nature*, 2023).
- Critically, the live-animal trade had been shut down and animals largely removed/culled before China CDC sampling began; animal samples were largely "unsold contents of refrigerators and freezers, swabs from stray animals and the contents of a fish tank," not live market animals (Liu et al., 2023). So the zero-positive result reflects what was left to test, not a clean test of live susceptible animals.
- Genetic (metagenomic) re-analysis of the market swabs confirmed that **DNA/RNA of SARS-CoV-2-susceptible species — raccoon dogs, civets, bamboo rats — co-occurred in SARS-CoV-2-positive samples**, including in the southwest corner wildlife stalls; several virus-positive samples carried raccoon-dog genetic material at high abundance (Crits-Christoph et al., *Cell*, 2024).
- But the same authors and independent analysts stress that **co-location of animal DNA with viral RNA does not prove any animal was infected** — virus on a cage or stall could come from infected humans, and samples were collected weeks after extensive human-to-human transmission (Débarre, *Virus Evolution*, 2024).
- Upstream supply-chain / wildlife-farm tracing in the WHO–China joint mission (2021) was inconclusive: no infected farmed or wild animal was identified, and the "intermediate host" pathway remained a hypothesis without a confirmed positive animal (WHO, 2021).

## Evidence quality
The core factual claim — no animal sample tested positive, and no infected source animal has been identified to date — rests on **peer-reviewed primary sources** (Liu et al. in *Nature*; Crits-Christoph et al. in *Cell*; Débarre in *Virus Evolution*) plus the official WHO–China report. These are independent and mutually consistent on the factual point. The claim is well established and largely uncontested.

What is contested is the **interpretation**: whether the absence is informative about origins. This is fundamentally an absence-of-evidence argument, and the evidence base has a strong confounder — the animals were removed and the market disinfected before sampling, so a clean test was never possible.

## Strongest counterargument
The natural-origin rebuttal is that the absence is fully expected and uninformative, not a genuine gap. (1) Susceptible species (raccoon dogs, civets) were genetically confirmed present in the exact stalls with the highest viral positivity (Crits-Christoph et al., 2024), so the spatial signal points where a spillover would. (2) Sampling occurred **after** the live animals had been culled/removed and surfaces cleaned, and weeks into sustained human transmission, destroying the window to catch an infected animal (Débarre, 2024). (3) For SARS-CoV-1 it took months and for MERS it took ~1–2 years to find the intermediate host even with the live trade intact and accessible — here the relevant animals and farms were depopulated under COVID controls before independent investigators had access. On this view, the negative result excludes essentially nothing, so it cannot weigh against zoonosis. (Conversely, lab-leak proponents counter that authorities had every incentive and capacity to find an infected animal and did not, and that the rapid culling itself is suspicious — but this is a circumstantial argument, not positive evidence.)

## Confidence assessment
- **Claim confidence:** 0.92 — The factual core (zero positive animal samples; no infected source animal identified to date) is solidly documented in multiple peer-reviewed primary sources. The small residual uncertainty reflects ongoing methodological disputes over the China CDC species assignments and the possibility of unreported sampling.
- **Support strength:** weak — For lab-leak. The absence is real, but it is heavily confounded by pre-sampling depopulation and disinfection, so it is close to non-diagnostic between hypotheses. It removes one piece of confirmatory evidence a zoonosis would ideally leave, without supplying any positive evidence of a lab origin.
- **Net:** A calibrated reasoner should treat "no positive source animal" as a true but only mildly informative fact: it modestly lowers confidence in a *fully confirmed* zoonotic chain, but because susceptible animals were demonstrably present and then removed before testing, it does not meaningfully raise the probability of a laboratory origin.
