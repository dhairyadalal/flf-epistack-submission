# RaTG13's Mojiang mine provenance and the WIV's delayed, incomplete disclosure

## Claim
RaTG13 — the bat coronavirus that in February 2020 was SARS-CoV-2's closest known relative (~96.1–96.2% genome identity) — was collected by the Wuhan Institute of Virology (WIV) from an abandoned mineshaft near Tongguan, Mojiang County, Yunnan, the same mine where six miners cleaning bat guano in 2012 developed severe pneumonia and three died. The WIV's February 2020 Nature paper (Zhou et al.) did not disclose that RaTG13 was the same sample previously published as BtCoV/4991, nor that it came from this mine, nor the miners' illness; these links were acknowledged only belatedly (notably in a November 2020 Nature addendum) after outside investigators reconstructed them.

## Key facts
- **The mine and the miners.** In April 2012, six men cleaning bat feces in an abandoned copper mineshaft near Tongguan, Mojiang, developed severe respiratory illness (cough, fever, ARDS, ground-glass opacities); three died over roughly the following months. This is documented in a 2013 Kunming Medical University master's thesis and reconstructed by Rahalkar & Bahulikar (2020).
- **Sample provenance.** The WIV/Shi Zhengli group sampled the mine multiple times between August 2012 and July 2013, recovering ~150 alphacoronaviruses and two betacoronaviruses, one of which was a SARS-like betacoronavirus designated BtCoV/4991 (Ge et al. 2016 reported the partial RdRp sequence of 4991).
- **The renaming.** RaTG13 is the same sample as BtCoV/4991 / Ra4991 (Rahalkar & Bahulikar 2020; confirmed in the Zhou et al. addendum). "RaTG13" encodes *Rhinolophus affinis* (Ra), Tongguan (TG), and 2013 (year). The WIV's Feb 2020 paper used the new name without flagging the identity with the already-published 4991, which obscured the mine link.
- **Sequencing timing.** The November 2020 addendum states the near-full genome of RaTG13 was sequenced in 2018, i.e., before the pandemic — not generated de novo in 2020.
- **The serum retesting.** Per the addendum, in 2020 the WIV retested archived miner serum samples by ELISA against the SARS-CoV-2 nucleocapsid protein; all results were negative for SARS-CoV-2 antibodies. (The 2013 thesis had separately reported testing the miners for SARS-related antibodies.)
- **Database takedown.** The WIV's public sample/sequence database went offline on 12 September 2019. Shi later told the BBC this was due to cyberattacks/security concerns. The timing (months before the outbreak) is cited by lab-leak proponents as suspicious; it is not itself proof of anything.
- **Delayed disclosure is real but bounded.** The substantive point that holds up: the Feb 2020 paper did not connect its headline virus to the mine, the deaths, or the prior 4991 publication; those connections became public mainly through external sleuthing and were confirmed by the WIV only afterward.

## Evidence quality
Mixed but largely solid on the factual core. The provenance facts (mine, miners, 4991=RaTG13, 2018 sequencing) are confirmed by the WIV's own peer-reviewed-journal addendum (Nature, 2020) and corroborated by independent peer-reviewed commentary (Rahalkar & Bahulikar, Frontiers in Public Health, 2020) and primary Chinese-language theses. This is strong documentary evidence. The *interpretation* — that the delay reflects a deliberate cover-up of dangerous research rather than ordinary opacity, citation lag, or pandemic chaos — is circumstantial and contested. The serum-retesting claim is a single-lab result reported without full raw data/controls, so it is moderately but not fully independent.

## Strongest counterargument
Delayed disclosure does not equal engineering or a leak. The WIV's 2020 retesting of the miners' sera against SARS-CoV-2 nucleocapsid was negative, undercutting the strongest version of the story (that the miners had a SARS-CoV-2-like infection that was then studied at WIV). The 2012 illness was never genetically confirmed as a SARS-related coronavirus; Shi Zhengli has stated a fungal pneumonia (and other diagnoses such as *Histoplasma*) was considered, and the master's-thesis attribution to a bat SARSr-CoV was a clinical inference, not virological proof. Citation/renaming opacity is common in virology, and the database takedown has a stated benign explanation (cyberattacks). Critically, RaTG13 at ~96% identity is far too distant (decades of evolution) to be SARS-CoV-2's direct progenitor, so its mere presence at WIV does not establish a leak. (See Speciale 2021 commentary, Frontiers in Public Health; Zhou et al. 2020 addendum.)

## Confidence assessment
- **Claim confidence:** 0.9 — The core factual claims (mine provenance, the 4991/RaTG13 identity, the miners' deaths, and that the Feb 2020 paper disclosed none of this while the addendum did so later) are confirmed by the WIV's own publication and multiple independent sources. The remaining uncertainty is mostly in characterizing intent and in the unverifiable details of the serum data.
- **Support strength:** moderate — for the lab-leak hypothesis. It establishes that WIV possessed and studied SARS-related, mine-derived coronaviruses and was less than forthcoming, which is necessary background for any research-related-incident scenario and damages the WIV's credibility as a narrator. But it does not demonstrate possession of a SARS-CoV-2 progenitor, gain-of-function on it, or any breach; the negative serum retest cuts the other way.
- **Net:** A calibrated reasoner should treat the Mojiang provenance and disclosure timeline as well-established fact that legitimately lowers trust in the WIV's early account and keeps a research-origin hypothesis live — but not as direct evidence that SARS-CoV-2 came from the lab, since RaTG13 is far too divergent to be its ancestor and the relevant serology came back negative.
