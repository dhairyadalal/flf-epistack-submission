# WIV handled novel SARS-related bat coronaviruses at BSL-2, and Shi Zhengli's request for higher containment was reportedly denied

## Claim
Documents and statements indicate that the Wuhan Institute of Virology (WIV) cultured and manipulated some novel SARS-related bat coronaviruses at biosafety level 2 (BSL-2) and BSL-3 rather than the highest containment (BSL-4). Separately, a 2018 U.S. State Department cable reported that Shi Zhengli's request to study SARS-related work in the WIV's new BSL-4 facility was denied by China's National Health and Family Planning Commission, with no reason given. The DEFUSE proposal (2018) further shows the WIV/EcoHealth team planned novel-coronavirus engineering work at BSL-2.

## Key facts
- **WIV's own statements confirm BSL-2/BSL-3 use for bat coronavirus work.** In a 2020 *Science* Q&A, Shi Zhengli stated her group used BSL-2 and BSL-3 laboratories for coronavirus research, only moving to BSL-4 after the pandemic per new government rules (Cohen / *Science*, 2020). This is the strongest, most direct confirmation of the core fact.
- **The 2018 State Department cable.** Two cables from U.S. diplomats who visited the WIV, first reported by Josh Rogin (*Washington Post*, April 2020), noted "a serious shortage of appropriately trained technicians and investigators needed to safely operate this high-containment laboratory." A later-unredacted passage (obtained via FOIA by U.S. Right to Know) reported that the National Health and Family Planning Commission denied Shi Zhengli permission to use the BSL-4 lab for SARS work, with "no reason given," while WIV scientists were still "allowed to study the SARS-like coronaviruses isolated from bats" — i.e., at lower containment.
- **DEFUSE proposal and Baric's comment.** A 2018 draft of the EcoHealth-led DEFUSE proposal to DARPA described novel-coronavirus engineering and testing at BSL-2 (advertised as "highly cost-effective"). Ralph Baric annotated the draft noting that in the US such recombinant SARS-CoVs are studied at BSL-3, adding that in China they "might be growin[g] these virus[es] under bsl2" and "US re[s]earchers will likely freak out." (Released documents, reported by U.S. Right to Know and the House Oversight Committee, 2024.)
- **Permitted at the time.** Under 2018-era norms, BSL-2 was an allowable level for viruses not known to transmit between humans. The work was not, by itself, a regulatory violation.

## Evidence quality
Mixed but largely solid for the **factual core**:
- The BSL-2/BSL-3 use is confirmed by Shi Zhengli herself in a peer-edited *Science* interview — high reliability.
- The DEFUSE/Baric material is from authentic released primary documents and is well attested.
- The State Department cable exists and is genuine, but its *interpretation* is contested (see counterargument). The most-cited "warning" framing comes from opinion journalism (Rogin), not the cable's own conclusions.
This is documentary/biosafety evidence, not genomic or epidemiological. It establishes a *capability/opportunity* (a plausible accident pathway), not that an accident occurred.

## Strongest counterargument
The biosafety facts are largely true but **weakly probative of origin**, and the "warning" framing is disputed. Zichen Wang (Pekingnology) and virologists including Angela Rasmussen argued Rogin mischaracterized the cable: it called the WIV "state-of-the-art," framed the staffing issue as a "talent gap" limiting productivity (not an imminent danger), and was overall impressed by WIV's output. Critically, BSL-2 culture of *known* bat coronaviruses was standard and permitted because those viruses were not established human pathogens; the DEFUSE BSL-2 plan was never funded; and the BSL-4 denial concerned *SARS (the original 2003 virus)*, a regulatory restriction, not evidence of reckless practice. Most importantly, no document places SARS-CoV-2 or a direct progenitor in any WIV freezer at any containment level — so BSL-2 handling shows *means*, not the *event*. Natural-origin proponents note this evidence is fully consistent with a lab that never had the pandemic virus.

## Confidence assessment
- **Claim confidence:** 0.85 — That WIV handled some SARS-related bat coronaviruses at BSL-2/BSL-3, and that Shi's BSL-4 SARS request was denied, is supported by her own statements, the cable, and DEFUSE. The residual uncertainty is mainly about precise scope (which specific viruses, which exact procedures) and the contested editorial framing of the cable.
- **Support strength:** weak — It establishes a plausible accident *mechanism* and opportunity, which modestly raises the prior on lab-leak. But it is circumstantial: it does not connect any BSL-2 work to SARS-CoV-2, its progenitor, or any infected worker. Means without evidence of the event.
- **Net:** A calibrated reasoner should accept that WIV did relevant work at lower containment than some Western labs would use, treat this as a real but generic enabling condition for an accidental leak, and resist the inflated "warning of imminent danger" gloss that opinion coverage attached to the underlying documents.
